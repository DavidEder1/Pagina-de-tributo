Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, born in 1914 in Cresco, Iowa, was a renowned scientist and humanitarian. He dedicated his life to fighting hunger and improving crop yields.

Throughout his career, Dr. Borlaug developed disease-resistant strains of wheat and introduced high-yielding varieties to countries facing food shortages. His work saved billions of lives and earned him numerous accolades, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.
